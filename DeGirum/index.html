Our Approach

We are enabling cloud quality AI at the edge by a combination of innovative hardware and intelligent software. The hardware is designed to take advantage of sparsity in pruned neural networks which reduces network model size, memory bandwidth and inference latency. The network compiler is designed to maximize data localization on the on-chip SRAM leading to lower memory bandwidth requirements and higher compute efficiency.

The HW is designed as a set of atomic compute and data movement primitives, which leaves the SW with the freedom to schedule any workload.

Our Architecture White Paper

Current AI applications at the edge are rather primitive due to the usage of toy models optimized for limited resource environment. Consumers demand much more sophisticated AI and are even willing to sacrifice their privacy for functionality.

The primary obstacle for the adoption of superior AI at the edge is the lack of HW that can run state-of-the-art networks within the power and cost constraints of the edge.

Our Technology
Network Pruning

It is well known that neural networks are heavily over parameterized and that pruned deep networks outperform their dense shallow counterparts. Exploiting pruned networks provides benefits along multiple dimensions: smaller model size, reduced compute load, lower memory bandwidth and lower energy.

DeGirum’s AutoSkip compute technology provides optimal support for pruned networks. This allows us to run deeper networks which deliver higher quality and performance.

Fractional Batching

Localizing filter and activation data on chip can increase HW efficiency but SRAM is an expensive resource. Our fractional batching technology schedules the computations in the order that maximizes data localization for a certain size of SRAM. Fractional batching allows running a part of the activation vector through multiple layers in succession.

This contrasts with scheduling strategies that always run an activation vector through a layer fully before moving on to the next layer.

Atomic HW Units

Some of the upcoming solutions for edge AI rely on localizing the entire network parameters and activations on chip. Such solutions are incapable of supporting any combination of network and input size that exceeds a certain value. Other solutions rely heavily on mapping the entire network structure to the hardware and can be quite efficient in terms of power and performance.

However, such solutions sacrifice generality and impose too many restrictions on the type of networks that the application developers can choose. Our architecture gives the SW complete freedom to schedule computations on any type of network by abstracting the HW as a set of optimized primitives for compute and data movement operations.
