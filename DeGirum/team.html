The most important feature of the microprocessor development board was the ROM based built-in machine language monitor, or "debugger" as it was also sometimes called. Often the name of the board was related to the name of this monitor program, for example the name of the monitor program of the KIM-1 was "Keyboard Input Monitor", because the ROM based software allowed entry of programs without the rows of cumbersome toggle switches that older systems used. Winston

Creative director

1. In your 30 years experience in Semiconductor industry, you have been an early

employee in 4 successful startups (SanDisk, Rambus, Marvell, MoSys) so far. What does DeGirum share with these successful companies?

Distinguishing Technology, Expertise, Good Teamwork, Focus, Problem Solvers, Flexible mindset, dedication and tenacity.

2. Why do you think support for sparsity is going to be a huge differentiating technology?

No existing company has the technology to take effective computational

advantage of sparse nets. With this technology, there can be many-fold gains in performance, significant reduction in power, memory bandwidth requirement and cost. A processor with this technology can process powerful state-of-the-art neural networks without compromise, making higher quality and more functional products possible at the Edge.

3. What application of AI are you most excited about?

Not excited about running any particular application but excited about running multiple real time AI applications through one chip giving sophisticated and rich experience to the user. For example, a product concurrently using object detection, motion recognition, distance estimation and voice to command/text functions processed through our chip would be quite interesting.
